# Complaint Procedure — Electronics Service
**Document version**: 1.0 (example / MVP seed)
**Applies to**: All consumer electronics categories listed in the product categories list.

---

## 1. General Eligibility Rules

**RULE-C-01** — A complaint is eligible for processing if the equipment was purchased no more than **24 months** ago from the date of submission. Equipment purchased more than 24 months ago is not eligible unless a paid extended warranty is documented (not applicable in MVP).

**RULE-C-02** — A complaint must include a description of the fault. Submissions without a fault description are rejected at intake.

**RULE-C-03** — A complaint must include photographic evidence of the equipment's condition at the time of submission. A single image is required.

---

## 2. Damage Classification

**RULE-C-04** — Damage is classified into one of three categories:

| Classification | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Manufacturing defect** | Fault present from production: component failure, software flaw, assembly error not caused by the user |
| **User-caused damage** | Physical damage resulting from misuse, dropping, liquid exposure, or unauthorized modification |
| **Normal wear and tear** | Surface-level cosmetic degradation consistent with normal use (minor scratches, faded markings) |

**RULE-C-05** — Only complaints classified as **manufacturing defect** are eligible for acceptance under the standard warranty.

**RULE-C-06** — Complaints classified as **user-caused damage** are rejected. The employee must communicate the reason clearly and inform the customer of paid repair options.

**RULE-C-07** — **Normal wear and tear** alone does not constitute grounds for a complaint. If the functional fault is due to a manufacturing defect alongside cosmetic wear, the complaint may still be accepted based on the manufacturing defect.

---

## 3. Specific Equipment Rules

**RULE-C-08 — Screens and displays**: A cracked screen is classified as user-caused damage unless there is evidence of a manufacturing defect (e.g. dead pixels, backlight failure, lines on display without physical impact).

**RULE-C-09 — Liquid damage**: Any evidence of liquid exposure (corrosion, moisture indicators triggered, condensation inside the unit) results in automatic rejection as user-caused damage.

**RULE-C-10 — Batteries**: Battery capacity degradation below 80% of original within the first 12 months qualifies as a manufacturing defect. Degradation after 12 months is considered normal wear.

**RULE-C-11 — Accessories** (cables, chargers, headphones): Complaint window is **12 months** from purchase, not 24 months.

**RULE-C-12 — Software issues**: Factory-resettable software faults are not accepted as complaints. The employee must first direct the customer to attempt a factory reset. If the fault persists after reset, it may be accepted as a manufacturing defect.

---

## 4. Decision Rules

**RULE-C-13** — Accept the complaint if ALL of the following are true:
- Purchase date is within 24 months (or 12 months for accessories).
- A fault description is provided.
- The image analysis confirms damage consistent with a manufacturing defect OR no visible damage is present but the reported fault is plausible.
- No evidence of user-caused damage or liquid exposure.

**RULE-C-14** — Reject the complaint if ANY of the following are true:
- Purchase date exceeds the eligible window.
- Image analysis confirms user-caused damage (physical impact, liquid damage).
- The fault reported has been resolved by a factory reset (employee must verify).

**RULE-C-15** — Issue a "Wymaga weryfikacji" (Requires verification) decision if:
- Image is ambiguous and cannot confirm or exclude user-caused damage.
- The reported fault does not match the visible condition in the image.
- The case falls into a category not covered by rules above.

---

## 5. Next Steps by Decision

| Decision | Next Steps for Employee |
|---|---|
| **Zaakceptowano** | Register the complaint in the service system. Issue a complaint reference number to the customer. Arrange equipment collection or in-store drop-off. Inform the customer of the estimated resolution time (14 business days standard). |
| **Odrzucono** | Communicate the rejection reason to the customer, citing the specific rule. Offer paid repair quote if applicable. Provide complaint appeal information if the customer disputes the decision. |
| **Wymaga weryfikacji** | Escalate the case to the service supervisor with a full case summary. Do not communicate a final decision to the customer until supervisor confirmation. Inform the customer that the case is under review and they will be contacted within 2 business days. |
